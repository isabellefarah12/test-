#GoalDiggin

Meet Natasha, a 19-year-old senior majoring in Political Science. Natasha is really in her 2nd year at UM but since she completed her Associate’s Degree in high school she will be finishing with her Bachelor’s Degree this May! Last summer, Natasha was #GoalDiggin through her internship with Judge Ellen Venzer in Florida’s 11th Judicial Circuit court. Natasha says, “My work involved redesigning the Judge’s “Hot Topics in Crime and Punishment” course and researching how implicit bias affects the criminal justice system. In return, she allowed me to observe court directly from the Judge’s bench.” She describes how having the opportunity to witness first-hand the unequal application of the law to certain sectors of society amplified her desire for change within the justice system.

Natasha explains how Toppel helped her when applying for law school, “Toppel’s Law Week provided workshops where I received an introduction to LSAT test prep, writing the personal statement, and was able to connect with an LSAC representative. Toppel's resources helped me to put forward a well-prepared application that ultimately resulted in my acceptance to and scholarships offers from four of the top 50 Law Schools in the country.”

A piece of advice from Natasha is: “Maintain a positive mindset. Focus on what you can control even if it's just uplifting your emotional state. Taking this approach not only feels better but allows you to be more productive.”

Freedom Mortgage is hiring for their Entry Level Mortgage Advisor Program called the “First Flyer Development Program”. It is a yearlong paid training program to become a Licensed Loan Officer. This is a customer focused, inside sales position within the Call Center. Participants will have a chance to develop rapport-building and sales skills, master software proficiency, and gain an understanding of operations within the mortgage industry. The First Flyer Development Program is a combination of both classroom and live sales training to give entry level students exposure to Freedom Mortgage in a fun, engaging, hands on environment!

Jennifer Perez

Assistant Director of Campus Talent

I graduated from Florida International University during summer of 2015. I had many interests and jumped around a few majors. Feeling lost, I looked towards the career center at FIU for a sense of direction. The career center at FIU provided me with the tools and resources I needed to design my path. Eager to graduate, I decided to major in Interdisciplinary Studies, minored in Hotel/Lodging Management and utilized my connections to land a campus position as a student assistant at The Office of International Affairs; the job that jumpstarted my career in higher education. I didn’t know it then but every person I met on my journey throughout my undergrad led me one step closer to where I am now.
